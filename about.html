About me

Evaluator. Methodologist.
Data Scientist.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Agricultural Education and Communication at the University of Florida (IFAS), with a minor in Research and Evaluation Methodology, working under the supervision of Dr. Walter Leite. My research sits at the intersection of program evaluation, behavior change, and empirical machine learning.

I design and analyze quasi-experimental and survey studies, applying structural equation modeling, latent class analysis, and machine-learning–based causal inference to large datasets in education and Extension — including a national longitudinal cohort study on COVID-19 and college outcomes, and multi-state text-mining work on citrus greening across four land-grant universities.

I also build AI and NLP tools — chatbots, text-mining pipelines, and LLM agents — to extend what evaluation can do. Before my doctoral studies, I served as a government Agricultural Extension Officer in Nepal, designing and evaluating more than a dozen extension programs, which keeps me grounded in the distance between what research recommends and what practitioners can use.
