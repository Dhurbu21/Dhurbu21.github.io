About

An evaluation methodologist working where behavioral science meets practice.

I am a doctoral candidate in Agricultural Education and Communication at the University of Florida, with a minor in Research and Evaluation Methodology. My work centers on program evaluation, evaluation capacity building (ECB), and the human dimensions of behavior within Cooperative Extension.

Methodologically, I work across advanced quantitative designs — structural equation modeling, confirmatory factor analysis, hierarchical regression, propensity score methods, and quasi-experiments — alongside qualitative and mixed-methods approaches. Increasingly, I am building an independent agenda at the intersection of artificial intelligence and evaluative thinking: asking what it means to reason evaluatively, and whether that capacity can be measured, benchmarked, and taught.

Before my doctoral work, I served in government agricultural extension in Nepal — experience that keeps me grounded in the gap between what research recommends and what practitioners can actually use.

Research

Building the capacity to evaluate — and to use what evaluation reveals.

My dissertation examines evaluation capacity building among Florida Cooperative Extension professionals (n = 143). A central finding: organizational ECB mediates the effect of individual ECB on two practice outcomes — the use of evaluation results and the mainstreaming of evaluation into routine work. Capacity, in other words, is not only a property of people; it lives in the structures around them.

Dissertation structural model — individual ECB → organizational ECB → evaluation use & mainstreaming (UF/IFAS, n = 143)

A program of work: can evaluative thinking be measured and taught — including in AI?

STUDY 01

Conceptual

A construct-attribution analysis of what "evaluative thinking" actually denotes across the literature.

STUDY 02

Instrument

Developing and validating a measure of evaluative thinking suitable for research and practice.

STUDY 03

AI Benchmarking

Benchmarking large language models against the same evaluative-reasoning criteria applied to people.

STUDY 04

Intervention

An experimental test of whether evaluative thinking can be deliberately built through training.
