Research

Building the capacity to evaluate — and to use what evaluation reveals.

Program evaluation, behavior change, and AI for agricultural education, communication, leadership, and Extension.

My dissertation examines evaluation capacity building (ECB) among Florida Cooperative Extension professionals (n = 143), validating the Evaluation Capacity Assessment Instrument. A central finding: organizational ECB mediates the effect of individual ECB on practice outcomes — the mainstreaming of evaluation. Capacity is not only a property of people; it lives in the structures around them.

Dissertation structural model — individual ECB → organizational ECB → evaluation use & mainstreaming (UF/IFAS, n = 143)

Research lines

Three connected threads.

LINE 01

Evaluation capacity building

Why evaluators and organizations in Cooperative Extension succeed or fail at building durable evaluation capacity.

LINE 02

AI, ML & NLP

Causal and predictive ML for educational data; chatbots, text mining, and NLP across multi-state USDA-NIFA work, including citrus greening (HLB).

LINE 03

Behavior change & methodology

SEM, latent class analysis, propensity score methods, and quasi-experimental designs to explain and change behavior in applied settings.

Emerging agenda

Can evaluative thinking be measured and taught — including in AI?

A four-study arc bridging measurement, AI benchmarking, and intervention.

STUDY 01

Conceptual

A construct-attribution analysis of what "evaluative thinking" actually denotes across the literature.

STUDY 02

Instrument

Developing and validating a measure of evaluative thinking for research and practice.

STUDY 03

AI Benchmarking

Benchmarking large language models against the same evaluative-reasoning criteria applied to people.

STUDY 04

Intervention

An experimental test of whether evaluative thinking can be deliberately built through training.
